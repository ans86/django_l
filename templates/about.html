AOT About


Attack on Titan (進撃の巨人 / Shingeki no Kyojin) is a Japanese manga and anime series created by "Hajime Isayama". It is one of the most popular and influential anime of all time, known for its dark themes, intense action, and deep story.

Story Summary:
In a world where *giant humanoid creatures called Titans* roam the earth and devour humans, the remnants of humanity live behind enormous concentric walls for protection. The story follows "Eren Yeager", a boy who vows to destroy all Titans after they destroy his hometown and kill his mother.

Main Themes:
- Freedom vs. oppression
- Humanity's survival
- War and its consequences
- Mystery and betrayal
- Political corruption

Main Characters:
- Eren Yeager – The protagonist, determined to wipe out the Titans.
- Mikasa Ackerman – Eren’s adoptive sister and elite fighter.
- Armin Arlert – Eren’s best friend, intelligent and strategic.
- Levi Ackerman – Humanity’s strongest soldier.
- Erwin Smith – Commander of the Scout Regiment.
- Zeke Yeager – A mysterious Titan shifter tied to Eren’s past.
- Reiner Braun, Annie Leonhart, and others with hidden identities.

Setting:
The world is divided into zones protected by three walls: Wall Maria, Wall Rose, and Wall Sheena. Outside the walls lie the Titans and the hidden truths about the world.

Manga & Anime Info:
Manga: Ran from 2009 to 2021 (34 volumes)
Anime: Aired from 2013 to 2023
Studios: Wit Studio (Seasons 1–3), MAPPA (Final Season)
Final Season split into multiple parts due to the story’s complexity

Notable Elements:
- Titan Shifters: Humans who can transform into powerful Titans.
- The Founding Titan: Holds the key to the origins and control of all Titans.
- Marley & Eldia: Two warring nations that reveal the broader political and racial conflict beyond the walls.

Why It's Loved:
- Deep and evolving plot
- Unpredictable twists
- Incredible animation and sound design
- Strong character development
- Philosophical and moral dilemmas

Themes of the Ending:
- Freedom vs Morality: Eren chose freedom at the cost of morality. Was he right or wrong?
- Cycle of Hatred: Even with Titans gone, hatred between people might still return.
- Tragic Hero: Eren becomes what he hated, but he believes it was the only way.
